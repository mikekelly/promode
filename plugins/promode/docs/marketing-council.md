# Marketing council: a dispatch pattern for productive disagreement

Routed mechanics doc — the `senior-marketer` def directs a read of this file when a marketing decision is high-stakes enough to warrant a **simulated advisory bench**: seating multiple expert lenses to collide over a call before committing to it. This is a **dispatch pattern, not a role** (there is no "council" agent) — it describes how to run a bench of persona-simulations and synthesise their disagreement. Cold-readable and opinion-dense.

Provenance: distilled 2026-07-15 from the `marketing-council` skill of coreyhaines31/marketingskills (MIT, Corey Haines), reference file `advisor-template.md` and the advisor dossiers. Mining ≠ endorsing.

<value-is-the-disagreement>
**The bench exists for the *disagreement*, not the consensus — an agreeing council is a mirror, not a board.** The whole design is anti-sycophancy (the marketing-lane sibling of O14's parallel-peer-answers and R4's consensus-audit):
- **Seat lenses that collide** — pick advisors whose frameworks genuinely conflict on this decision, not five variations of the same take.
- **Always seat a designated dissenter**, and **pre-wire named antagonists at construction** — decide who will push back *before* you see the answer, so the disagreement isn't optional.
- **Require every dossier to carry documented blind spots and ≥1 unpopular position** — an advisor with no blind spot and no unpopular take is a flattering echo, not a lens.
- **The deliverable is the disagreement map** — each conflict named as its *underlying trade-off* plus *what evidence would settle it* — not a merged "the council recommends." The value is the surfaced tension the main agent (and user) then adjudicate.
</value-is-the-disagreement>

<simulation-grounding>
The integrity envelope that makes persona-simulation usable at all — **simulation is labeled as simulation, grounded in documented positions only:**
- **No fabricated quotes.** Never state or imply that a real person *endorses* the user's product.
- **A living advisor's stored positions are a snapshot** — run a research pass for anything recent before leaning on their view. *(⌛ living advisors' positions drift by design — re-verify recent stances.)*
- **Never launder an advisor's self-reported marketing claims into facts**, and **attribute borrowed frameworks to their true originators.**
- **Private-person advisors get positions only from the user, never invented.**
</simulation-grounding>

<decides-not-executes>
**The council sets *direction*; it never writes the landing page.** Once direction is set, hand off to execution (the `senior-marketer` and the specialism docs). Scope gates:
- **Match bench size to stakes** — twelve advisors on a headline is the anti-pattern; reserve a full bench for genuine one-way doors.
- **Name-flavored generic advice isn't a take** — if an advisor's contribution survives swapping their name for another's, it's filler, not a lens.
- A crucial, hard-to-reverse *strategic* output of a council session (a positioning bet, a category call) is a `chief-marketing-officer` ratification artifact, not something the bench itself commits — the bench informs the draft, the main agent ratifies it.
</decides-not-executes>

<cites>
A council session serves a specific decision that traces to a `docs/marketing/` strategy node; its disagreement map and the resolved direction are captured as a linked decision node (what was decided, what was rejected, why — the disagreement is exactly the decision log worth keeping). Advisor personas used here are simulation constructs, distinct from the product's real documented personas in `docs/product/PERSONAS.md`.
</cites>
